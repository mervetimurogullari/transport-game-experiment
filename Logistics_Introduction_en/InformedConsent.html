Study on coalitions

This study is conducted by the Tilburg School of Social and Behavioral Sciences and the Tilburg School of Economics and Management of Tilburg University. We are interested in how people form coalitions and how make decisions about themselves and others. In this study, you will take the role of a transportation company, negotiating with two other participants, representing other companies, to divide a sum of money. {{ if incentives }} The money earned in the game will be converted into a real monetary bonus at the end of the experiment. {{ endif }}

{{ if timers }}

Important: In this study, three participants will engage in a live negotiation process. For this reason, the interactive elements of the negotiation are timed. Each page should be completed within 5 minutes, which is more than enough time. A timer will be displayed on all pages to which this applies.

If, during the negotiation phase of the study, you cannot complete any page within {{ timeout_time_minutes }} minutes, the study will automatically be terminated for all participants. {{ if incentives }} If this happens, none of the participants will be able to obtain any bonus payment. {{ endif }} Thus, make sure that you have enough time to complete the study if you participate.

{{ endif }}

Duration and payment

This study will take about 20 minutes. You will receive a base fee of 5.00 Euros as compensation. As a participant, you will answer questions throughout the negotiation process, to understand how you make decisions. Sometimes, we ask the same questions again to understand possible changes in the negotiation. Note that during this study, you will interact live with two other participants. This means that you sometimes must wait for a while, for example, when you are being matched with other participants or when other participants are considering their next action. This waiting time is included in the study duration 20 minutes. Because the negotiations can differ in how long they take, there is some variation in how long it takes to finish the study. Depending on the outcome of the negotiation, the money that you earned during the study will be converted into a real monetary bonus that you receive in addition to the base fee of between 0 and 5.00 euros.

If you have questions regarding this study, please contact Merve Timuroğulları at m.timurogullari@tilburguniversity.edu.

Confidentiality and Questions

All data obtained from participants will be kept anonymous. Any information collected about you will be coded using a fake name (pseudonym) or initials and numbers, for example abc-123, etc. The information which has your identifiable information will be kept separately from the rest of your data. It will be stored in a password-protected file and only the researchers will have access to it. Once anonymized, the data may be made available to researchers via accessible data repositories and possibly used for novel purposes. The data will be stored for at least 10 years.

This study has been approved by the Ethics Review Board of Tilburg School of Social and Behavioral Sciences (RP1664). If you have any remarks or complaints regarding this research, you may contact the Ethics Review Board of Tilburg School of Social and Behavioral Sciences via ERB@tilburguniversity.edu.

Participation, Benefits and Risks

Participation in this study is completely voluntary. You have the right to withdraw at any time or refuse to participate entirely. If you desire to withdraw, please simply close your internet browser. There will be no negative consequences if you withdraw or refuse to participate. Risks are minimal for involvement in this study. It is very unlikely that answering the questions affects you emotionally or otherwise. You have the right, in principle, to request access to and rectify, erase, restrict or object to the processing of your personal data.

By agreeing to participate in the study, you voluntarily agree to participate in this study. This does not interfere with your right to withdraw from this study at any time without an explanation.

  • I had sufficient time to decide whether I would participate or not.
  • I know that participation is completely voluntary.
